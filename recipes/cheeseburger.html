Classic, Juicy Cheese Burger

Our Classic, Juicy Cheese Burger is all about comfort and flavor. A thick beef patty is grilled until it is tender and full of flavor, then topped with a generous slice of cheese that melts perfectly over the meat. Every bite is hearty and satisfying, just the way a good burger should be.

We serve it on a soft toasted bun with crisp lettuce, fresh tomato, sweet red onions, and a few pickles for that extra crunch. It is simple, wholesome, and made to remind you of the kind of burger you would enjoy at a family bbq or around the kitchen table.

Ingredients

  • 200 g ground beef, about 80/20 lean to fat
  • 1 burger bun, split and lightly toasted
  • 1 slice cheddar cheese, 20-25 g
  • Soft toasted burger bun
  • 1 crisp lettuce leaf
  • 1-2 slices tomato
  • 3-4 rings red onion
  • 3-4 pickle slices
  • 1 tbsp (15 ml) mayonnaise
  • 1 tsp (5 ml) butter or neutral oil for toasting the bun
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Prep the toppings. Slice the tomato, red onion, and pickles. Have your cheese slice ready.
  2. Shape the patty. Gently form the 200 g beef into a disc about 10 cm wide and roughly 2 cm thick. Make a small dimple in the centre with your thumb to help it cook flat. Do not overwork the meat.
  3. Toast the bun. Heat a pan on medium. Add the butter or oil and toast the cut sides of the bun until golden, 30-60 seconds. Set aside.
  4. Season just before cooking. Increase the pan to medium-high. Sprinkle salt and pepper on both sides of the patty.
  5. Cook the first side. Add a few drops of oil if the pan is dry. Place the patty in the pan and cook without pressing, about 4-5 minutes, until well browned.
  6. Flip and add cheese. Turn the patty and cook 3-4 minutes more. In the last minute, place the cheese on top. Add a teaspoon of water to the pan and cover to help the cheese melt.
  7. Check doneness. For safety, aim for an internal temperature of about 70 Â°C for ground beef. If you do not have a thermometer, look for clear juices and a firm but still juicy feel.
  8. Rest. Move the patty to a plate and rest 2 minutes so the juices settle.
  9. Build the burger. Spread the sauce on the bottom bun, add lettuce, the cheesy patty, tomato, red onion, pickles, then the top bun. Serve immediately.
